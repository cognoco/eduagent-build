# Your privacy at MentoMate

> **Draft for DPO review and testing with young people.** This is not the final published notice. The final version must be checked for comprehension, approved guardian-visibility wording, provider evidence, DPO contact details, and any required UK representative.

## The short version

MentoMate is an AI tutor. It remembers what you are learning so it can help you over time. You must be at least 13 to use it.

ZWIZZLY AS is responsible for looking after your personal information.

## What we know about you

We keep information you or your parent gives us, such as your name, date of birth, email address, language, and pronouns.

We also keep information about your learning: subjects, messages, progress, review results, notes, and what you have mastered or may need to practise again.

We do not collect your location, contacts, browsing history, or other photos from your device. We use a homework image only when you choose one. If you use the microphone, your device turns your voice into text; MentoMate does not receive or keep the audio recording.

## You are talking to AI

The mentor's replies are made by artificial intelligence, not a human teacher. The learning screen tells you this. AI can make mistakes, so you can question an answer, ask for a different explanation, or speak to a trusted adult.

## What “learning memory” means

MentoMate makes a learning memory from your activity. It may include subjects, progress, mastery results, notes, recaps, and short quotations from things you wrote. This helps the tutor remember what you have already learned.

We use this memory for tutoring continuity—not advertising, marketing profiles, or training another company's general-purpose AI model.

## Who receives information

MentoMate uses specialist companies to provide sign-in, AI responses, embeddings, hosting, email, error monitoring, notifications, and subscriptions. Some are outside Norway, the EU/EEA, or the United Kingdom, including in the United States.

When the learner is under 18, MentoMate removes their name and account identifiers before sending learning content to an AI model provider. The learning content and context needed to answer can still be sent.

The final notice will link to the reviewed provider list and explain the legal safeguards used for international transfers.

## How long information stays

The full text of a learning chat is normally deleted 30 days after its learning summary is generated. If summary processing is delayed, the system raises an operational alert so the delay can be resolved.

The learning memory stays while the account is active. Deleting an account starts a seven-day period in which deletion can be cancelled. After that, personal and learning data is removed, except for limited records that must be kept for legal, security, fraud-prevention, or accounting reasons.

If a parent withdraws consent, consent can be restored during the seven-day grace period. After that, the profile is deleted or, for some older learners and depending on the account owner's setting, archived for 30 more days before deletion.

## Parents and guardians

A parent or guardian may manage consent and see parts of a managed learner's progress. The final summary must state exactly what guardians can see after the controller and DPO complete the child-best-interests assessment; this draft does not guess that scope.

## Your choices and rights

You can ask what information MentoMate has about you, ask for inaccurate information to be corrected, object to some uses, withdraw consent, request a portable copy, or ask for deletion. Some account actions must be completed by the account owner or guardian.

Use the privacy controls in the app or contact `support@mentomate.com`. The final version will also include the appointed DPO's confirmed contact details.

## Comprehension prompts for consultation

Use these questions during age-appropriate testing; record where the wording fails and revise it.

1. Who are you talking to during a MentoMate learning session?
2. What is the learning memory, and why does MentoMate keep it?
3. What information can be sent to an AI provider?
4. How long is the full chat text normally kept?
5. What happens after an account-deletion request?
6. What can you do if information about you is wrong or you want it deleted?
7. What do you think a parent or guardian can see? Was the explanation clear?
