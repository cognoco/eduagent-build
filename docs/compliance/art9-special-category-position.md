# Article 9 special-category data position

**Status:** Proposed controller position for DPO/privacy-counsel advice<br>
**As of:** 24 July 2026<br>
**Scope:** MentoMate consumer service

## Position

MentoMate does not ask learners to provide special-category data and does not
intend to use such data for tutoring personalisation, learning assessment,
advertising, or model training.

Because learners can write freely, they may nevertheless disclose information
about health, disability, religion, political views, ethnicity, sexual
orientation, or other sensitive matters. An AI system may also infer such
information. Incidental disclosure, inference, transmission, and temporary
storage are therefore foreseeable and must not be described categorically as
impossible.

## Controller safeguards

ZWIZZLY AS’s proposed safeguards are to:

- discourage unnecessary disclosure in clear, child-readable language;
- prevent prompts and product features from soliciting or unnecessarily
  inferring sensitive information;
- exclude sensitive characterisations from persistent learning memory,
  profiling, assessment, and guardian-facing summaries;
- retain incidental sensitive content only for the shortest justified period;
- prevent use for advertising or model training; and
- handle genuine crisis content through a separate safeguarding procedure.

The distinction is between discussing a sensitive topic and creating a
sensitive characterisation of the learner. Educational discussion is not
automatically a learner health or disability record. A durable statement such
as “this learner is likely dyslexic” is a sensitive inference and is outside
the intended product purpose.

## Advice required

The DPO/privacy adviser is asked to confirm:

1. whether the distinction between intended use and incidental disclosure or
   inference is legally sound;
2. whether foreseeable incidental handling constitutes Article 9 processing;
3. which Article 9 condition, if any, is required; and
4. whether the proposed minimisation, suppression, retention, and
   safeguarding measures are sufficient.

The final DPIA and ROPA must reflect that advice. The historical categorical
“no Article 9 processing” decision is retained under
[`history/`](history/2026-06-08-art9-no-processing-decision-superseded.md) only
as provenance.
