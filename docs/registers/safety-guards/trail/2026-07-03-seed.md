# Trail record — seed the safety-guards register

**Date:** 2026-07-03
**Change:** Created `docs/registers/safety-guards/master.md` and seeded it
with four rows.
**WI:** WI-1353 (Document justified prompt-only safety guards in a safety
register)

## Why

The **WI-1285** audit inventoried every prompt-only safety rule in
`apps/api/src/services/exchange-prompts.ts`'s SAFETY block (eight sites),
classifying each as either acceptable to remain **prompt-only** or as
needing a **server-side gate**. Two sites were routed to follow-up Work
Items and got deterministic gates:

- **WI-1154** — dangerous-procedure operational how-to leak
  (`apps/api/src/services/dangerous-procedure-gate.ts`), triggered by eval
  probe SL-DU02 leaking opium→heroin extraction steps to a minor via a weak
  fallback model.
- **WI-1348** — minor-PII echo-back into the persisted `ai_response.content`
  (`apps/api/src/services/minor-pii-echo-gate.ts`), a GDPR-K data-protection
  concern.

Two other sites were judged genuinely acceptable as prompt-only by the
WI-1285 audit and were **not** routed to a gate follow-up:

- Jailbreak / system-prompt exfiltration / roleplay refusal
  (`exchange-prompts.ts:695`) — the system prompt holds no secrets, and
  deterministic jailbreak detection is high-false-positive against
  legitimate tutoring roleplay.
- Slur-explanation-without-repetition (`exchange-prompts.ts:689-691`) — low
  stakes, and answering the learner's question first is the correct UX; a
  deterministic gate risks over-triggering on ordinary vocabulary
  questions.

Without a durable record, a future safety audit re-scanning
`exchange-prompts.ts`'s SAFETY block would re-flag these two prompt-only
sites as unreviewed gaps, re-litigating a decision the WI-1285 audit already
made. This register exists to prevent that, and to hold both the gated and
the justified-prompt-only outcomes together as one ledger.
