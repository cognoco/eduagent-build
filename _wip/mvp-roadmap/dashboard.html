A program this size feels unmanageable when you watch it at the wrong altitude. This page exists so a PM and an orchestrator can drive one product to launch without holding any of it in their heads.

01The shape: four altitudes, watch one

Since the ZDX reboot (2026-07-22), all delivery runs through Delivery Batches. Each level below is somebody else's job to track, except one.

ProgramMentoMate1 product, one destination: public launch. Operator + PM steer.
Delivery batchBID-NN lanesA shepherd-run cluster of work with one theme — “mentor-notice”, “run-smoke cure”. This is your altitude.
Shepherd laneOne driver per batchA shepherd (claude or codex) drives a batch's items to green: claim → build → review → merge.
Work itemCosmo WIsHundreds. Agents claim, execute, and close them. You never need the list.

Batches are stable — a work item churns hourly, a batch changes only when its theme is delivered. If you can say "the mentor-notice batch is on its final audit, run-smoke cure is chasing the root cause", you have full situational awareness.

02Nobody picks from a hat: the launch chain sequences everything

The program has no calendar and no priority meetings. Toward launch, sequencing is done by one ordered chain — the launch chain on the Sequence page: compliance closure → store declarations → publishing → production dogfood → closed beta → public launch. Each link is a real Work Item, and most are operator-gated (an OPQ) or counsel-gated (a legal clock).

So the answer to "what's between us and launch?" is never an opinion — it's the six links on the rail, and which OPQ each one waits behind.

Only three things ever need a human decision: ① an operator queue ruling (store creds, staging windows, product one-liners); ② a legal clock — DPO retainer, Art-28 DPAs, retention periods, the counsel packet; ③ the public-launch go/no-go once the chain is green. Everything else is a shepherd or an agent's job.

03A batch is a recipe, not a project

A Delivery Batch is formed from a themed brief — its scope and which shepherd runtime drives it. We deliberately do not pre-slice everything into work items ahead of time (plans go stale; the brief is the right level until the work is real). Once formed, the lane is mechanical:

STEP 1
FormA brief + a shepherd lane. The batch appears as a card the moment it's formed.
STEP 2
ClaimThe shepherd claims the batch's items in Cosmo, setting order and dependencies.
STEP 3
ShepherdAgents claim → build → review → merge, item by item. You watch at batch level.
STEP 4
GraduateWhen every item is Closed, the batch graduates — kept on the board for the record.

The batches that came first (identity cutover, the QA factory drains) proved the machinery. Every later batch reuses the pattern.
