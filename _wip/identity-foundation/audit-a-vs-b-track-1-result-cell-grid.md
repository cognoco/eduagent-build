## Framing: Option A vs Option B

The identity-foundation decision is whether **v1 serves all three consent categories** (Option A) or **defers Category 3 to a demand-triggered phase 2** (Option B). The three categories are the canon's consent taxonomy primitives:

- **Cat 1** — adult: consent-capable, `payerEligible`, age ≥ 18; self-consents.
- **Cat 2** — consent-capable minor: above the jurisdictional digital-consent age (13–16, shipped strictest 16) but under 18; self-consents, NOT `payerEligible`.
- **Cat 3** — consent-gated minor: below the digital-consent age; `requiresGuardianConsent`, consent flows from a guardian.

Each cell compares what the **canon** (`_wip/identity-foundation/` PRD Part 10 §I, `identity-ontology.md`, `domain-model.md`, `data-model.md`, ADRs, plus `CLAUDE.md`/`AGENTS.md` governance prose) prescribes against what the **code** actually does, then tags whether the surface is **A-only** (a Cat-3-specific surface that Option B removes — a "B win"), **B-only** (a Cat-2 / consent-capable surface Option B must preserve), or **both** (category-agnostic).

The dominant finding: the code today has **no category model at all**. The age model is binary (`adolescent < 18` / `adult ≥ 18` via `computeAgeBracket`, theming-only), and consent is an age-only `≤16` GDPR cliff with an 11 floor — no jurisdiction input, no self-consenting-minor branch, no `consent_grant`/`consent_receipt`/`guardianship` tables, no time-triggered age-crossing sweep. Cat 2 is collapsed into Cat 3 (`B-only` drift), and most of the canon's identity machinery is `code lacks`.
