# Track 3 — Layered Cross-Check (re-slice Tracks 1 and 2 by layer)

**Audit:** Identity Foundation — A-vs-B directional audit, Track 3 (final synthesis artefact)
**Date:** 2026-06-05
**Method:** Re-slice of Track 1's workstream-agnostic cell grid and Track 2's per-workstream estimates into a (layer × workstream) matrix. No re-walk of canon; the layer view is a re-projection of Tracks 1 and 2, with disagreements between the two views surfaced.

**The decision:** The PM is choosing the v1 launch scope for the identity-foundation stream. **Option A** ships all three consent categories in v1 (Cat-1 adult self-consent; Cat-2 minor-able-to-consent self-consent; **Cat-3 minor-not-able-to-consent**, whose consent flows from a guardian under Art 8(2) / COPPA-under-13). **Option B** ships Cat-1 + Cat-2 only and defers Cat-3 to v2 behind a module-level config flag. The architectural primitive is "1+2 only vs all three," not any specific launch-floor age. The canon was written assuming Option A, which is precisely why a code-anchored estimate is needed. This Track-3 file answers the layered question the workstream view cannot: **where in the stack do B's savings live, where is the dominant build cost, and where is the half-migration risk?**
