Identity Foundation — Conceptual Map

The core nouns and how they relate. A teaching aid for the B-product walkthrough.
This is NOT the data model. No tables, columns, or cardinalities (no “one-to-many” / “many-to-many”). It shows only the core concepts and their main relationships, to anchor the conversation. The real schema is a later phase.
Guardianship — who may consent for a child Mentorship — who may see / help a learner Payer — who pays (no data access)
The shift from today: right now one “account” is the login, the family, and the person all rolled into one — and “the owner” of the account runs it, pays for it, and is the parent, all at once. This map pulls those apart: a person and their sign-in are separate, the family is just a group that doesn’t own anyone, and helping with someone else’s learning is an explicit link (Guardianship or Mentorship), never automatic.
