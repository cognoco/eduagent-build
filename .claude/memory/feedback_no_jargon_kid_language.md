The app uses developer/product jargon that's unclear to real users:
- "Practice now" → practice what?
- "Open Learning Book" → what's a learning book?
- "Coaching card" → invisible to user but leaks into empty states
- "Curriculum" → formal/academic term most people don't use
- "Retention" → meaningless outside spaced-repetition context
- "Additional verification available" → confusing for anyone

**Why:** The app is for learners of ALL ages — kids through adults. The language must be universally clear, not dumbed down for kids or dressed up for adults. Good UX text is plain, direct, and jargon-free for everyone. The home screen should speak to real moments: "I have homework", "Help me study for a test", "I want to explore something new".

**How to apply:** When writing or reviewing any user-facing text, ask: "Is this instantly clear to someone who has never used the app?" If not, rewrite it. Prefer verbs over nouns ("I have homework" not "Homework help"). Prefer concrete over abstract ("Study for a test" not "Practice now"). Never expose internal concepts (coaching cards, retention, curriculum) in the UI.
