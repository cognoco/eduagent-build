MentoMate is not a kids-only app. It is for anyone who wants to study.

The core product distinction is:

- Student mode: the user is studying. A student can be a child student or an adult student.
- Parent/family mode: the user is reviewing, supporting, or managing a child's learning.
- Parent as student: a parent can also study for themselves and should have an easy path into the same learning experience adult students use.
- Child learner/profile: a child does not need to have a linked account or their own login to exist in the family learning model, but they can have their own learner account at any age.

Agreed IA direction as of 2026-05-19:

- Parent accounts land in Family Hub by default, because most parents primarily arrive to support their children.
- Parents need a very easy, warm path into My Learning, because parent-to-student activation is part of the product strategy.
- Once in My Learning, parents should get the same strong student experience as any adult learner.
- When a parent views a child, the app must visibly change context to "Viewing <child>", with limited actions and clear privacy boundaries.

**Why:** The user corrected a recurring agent misunderstanding on 2026-05-19. Agents keep flattening the product into a children's learning app with parent controls, which leads to bad UX recommendations.

**How to apply:** Do not frame parent learning as an edge case or child-mode proxy. Preserve an easy parent-to-learning path, but keep family review surfaces and active studying surfaces conceptually distinct. When discussing roles, use "student/learner" broadly and qualify child vs adult only when age, consent, guardian access, privacy, or linked-account status matters. Do not assume every child learner has a linked account, and do not assume learner accounts are adult-only or age-gated.
